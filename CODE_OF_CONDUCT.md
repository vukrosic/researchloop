# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want AutoResearch-AI to be a useful, welcoming project for researchers, engineers, students, and AI agents acting on their behalf. We pledge to make participation here a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful in issues, PRs, discussions, and reviews.
- Critique the code, the experiment, the result — not the person.
- Assume good intent. Ask before assuming malice or carelessness.
- Be patient with new contributors and with AI-agent-authored PRs (which still need a human reviewer of record).
- Cite sources. Don't claim results you didn't run.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, insults, or sustained disruption.
- Harassment in public or private.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Submitting fabricated experiment results or plagiarized code/text.
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the project, or low-effort AI-generated noise in issues / PRs / discussions.

## Scope

This code applies in all project spaces: the GitHub repo, issues, pull requests, discussions, project social accounts, and any in-person or online event organized under the project's name. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project elsewhere.

## Reporting

Report unacceptable behavior to **shifter040@gmail.com**. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer will respect the privacy and security of the reporter.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this code, and may temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.
